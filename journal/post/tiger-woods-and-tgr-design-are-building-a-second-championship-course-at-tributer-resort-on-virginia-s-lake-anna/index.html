Tiger Woods is building a second championship course at Tributer Resort

By Jake Nicholas June 28, 2026

Tiger Woods is bringing a championship course to the shores of Lake Anna. Tributer Resort, the golf and residential destination by Reef Capital Partners in central Virginia, has tapped Woods' TGR Design to build a new private, 18-hole championship course — the resort's second, and the latest addition to the firm's growing portfolio of marquee golf destinations.

The course will play to 7,310 yards from the championship tees, routed through rolling farmland, mature hardwood forests, and pine groves overlooking the lake. TGR Design has shaped it as a distinct core layout meant to showcase the property's dramatic topography, defined by strategic shot values, generous fairways, strategically placed hazards, and undulating greens that reward precision and imagination while staying playable for golfers of every level.

"A balanced mix of uphill and downhill holes, shifting perspectives, and natural landforms will ensure no two holes feel alike, creating a course that feels organic in its movement and varied in its demands," said Bryon Bell, President at TGR Design. "Lake Anna provides a remarkable setting for championship golf, and we're excited to see this design come to life in a way that fully reflects its sense of place."

The new course joins Cutalong Golf Club, Tributer's existing private course, which Golf Digest named one of the Top 5 Best New Private Courses in the United States in 2023 — a layout inspired by National Golf Links of America and the Golden Age principles of C.B. MacDonald, Donald Ross, and Alister MacKenzie.

Alongside it sits the Shenandoah Green, a lighted 18-hole putting course built as a social venue beside the clubhouse, which overlooks the 1st tee and 18th green and houses Coopers, the resort's signature dining venue and golf shop. "This new course announcement represents a major milestone in the evolution of Tributer Resort and reinforces our commitment to building a premier golf destination at Lake Anna," said Ian Sikes, General Manager of Tributer Resort.

Beyond the fairways, Reef Capital Partners is reimagining Tributer into a full lifestyle and residential community. The current collection includes homesites, golf villas, and cottages — all member-owned, with select homes available to guests through Stay & Play reservations — built in an architectural style that marries traditional Virginia design with contemporary comfort.

The four-bedroom Golf Villas, now selling, feature spa-inspired baths, open-concept living, and private terraces over the course, while two-bedroom Golf Cottages bring chef-inspired kitchens and outdoor porches; homesites run from one-third to two-thirds of an acre with golf-course views.

Once complete, the community will grow to nearly 900 residences alongside additional chef-driven dining, sports courts, wellness amenities, pool and lakeside recreation, and trail systems — a year-round resort environment rooted in golf, hospitality, and waterfront living.

"As we continue to expand Tributer Resort, our vision is to create a vibrant, enduring community where resort amenities are seamlessly integrated into everyday life," said Jared Lucero, CEO of Reef Capital Partners. With a second Tiger Woods-designed course now on the way, the case for Lake Anna as one of the East Coast's emerging golf addresses only grows stronger.
