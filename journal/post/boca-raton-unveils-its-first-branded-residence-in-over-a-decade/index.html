Boca Raton unveils its first branded residence in over a decade

Key International and Wexford Real Estate Investors have just unveiled Mr. C Residences Boca Raton, a 12-story boutique tower in the heart of Downtown Boca.

By Jake Nicholas January 27, 2026

Boca Raton is about to welcome its first newly announced branded residential tower in more than a decade.

Key International and Wexford Real Estate Investors have just unveiled Mr. C Residences Boca Raton, a 12-story boutique tower rising at 41 SE 4th Street in the heart of Downtown Boca. The project brings the Cipriani family’s hospitality-led Mr. C brand to one of South Florida’s most established luxury markets, marking a significant new chapter for the city’s residential landscape.

The tower will feature 133 private residences with two- and three-bedroom layouts, priced from $1.7 million. Sales are expected to launch this spring, led exclusively by Douglas Elliman Development Marketing, with an on-site sales gallery opening at 36 SE 3rd Street.

Designed by Arquitectonica with interiors by 1508 London, the building introduces a refined, European-inspired approach to downtown living. Homes will feature open kitchens with custom Italian cabinetry, natural stone countertops, and Wolf and Sub-Zero appliances, along with floor-to-ceiling windows and expansive terraces.

“Building on the success of Mr. C in Coconut Grove and West Palm Beach, the project will deliver the Cipriani family’s legacy in cultural and hospitality expertise, coupled with the modernity of the Mr. C Flag to Boca Raton" said Jay Phillip Parker, CEO of Brokerage, Florida Region and President of Douglas Elliman Development Marketing, Florida.

Amenities span multiple levels, anchored by a rooftop resort deck with an infinity pool, private cabanas, an open-air cinema, outdoor dining areas, and lounge spaces. A dedicated wellness level includes a full spa with hammam, infrared sauna, steam room, sound dome, fitness center, yoga studio, lap pool, hot and cold plunges, and private padel and pickleball courts.

Hospitality is woven throughout the experience, from the ground-floor Bellini Restaurant to the residents-only rooftop Mr. C Bar & Lounge. In-residence dining, poolside service, concierge, valet, housekeeping, and spa services complete the offering—extending the brand’s hotel DNA into daily life.

With Mr. C already established in Coconut Grove and West Palm Beach, the Boca Raton debut reinforces South Florida’s position as the country’s leading market for branded residences—and signals a new era for Downtown Boca’s luxury evolution.
