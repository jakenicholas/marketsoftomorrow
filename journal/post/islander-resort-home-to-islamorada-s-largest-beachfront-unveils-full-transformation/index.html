The Florida Keys

Islander Resort, home to Islamorada’s largest beachfront, unveils full transformation

Islamorada's historic, Islander Resort has completed a full-scale transformation, redefining the essence of Florida Keys vacations with...

By Jake Nicholas February 19, 2025

Islamorada's historic, Islander Resort has completed a full-scale transformation, redefining the essence of Florida Keys vacations with a complete interior and exterior renovation. Spanning 24 acres, the revitalized resort now features newly designed accommodations, enhanced resort grounds with endless activities, immersive guest experiences, and elevated dining options - all inspired by the natural beauty and vibrant culture of the Florida Keys.

The heart of the resort’s transformation lies in its refreshed bungalows, suites and two-bedroom townhome-style Bayside Villas. Thoughtfully redesigned with a harmonious blend of tropical ease and contemporary sophistication, these spacious retreats bring coastal-inspired interiors, modern appliances, and expanded patios - truly bringing a space that is accommodating for even the longest stays. Twelve new Cabana Poolside Rooms offer expanded outdoor living spaces with exclusive poolside cabanas and dedicated service.

For those seeking extended stays or boating adventures, the Bayside Villas provide the comforts of home with gourmet kitchens, spacious living areas, and private decks, all with access to the Sunset Pool, Emerald Beach, and boat slips.

Not only are the homes accomodating, but the Islander Resort also provides free shuttle service to breweries, restaurants, and Islamorada's top sightseeing attractions. For those seeking a free ride off of Islamorada, The Florida Key's free ride service, Freebee is a great option for private rides.

Designed in collaboration with Gensler, EDSA landscape architects, Theresa Fatino Design, KPR Consulting, and Destination Decoded, the resort’s enhancements embrace its storied past while introducing fresh, modern touches.

Set along Islamorada’s largest beachfront, Islander Resort invites guests to immerse themselves in the natural rhythm of the Keys. As evening falls, Firelight Cove becomes the heart of the resort, where guests gather for starlit storytelling, ocean breezes, and s’mores by the fire. Sunrise Pier, a tranquil vantage point over the water, is the perfect spot for marine life sightings and launching excursions to the world’s third-largest barrier reef.

Active travelers can enjoy the two-acre Seaside Sports area, featuring pickleball, basketball, volleyball, bocce ball, and a 9-hole Seaside Links putting course designed by PuttTek. Complimentary kayaking, paddleboarding, and snorkeling excursions are also available, with an everyday resort schedule bringing beachside painting, yoga, races, and more.

With over 129,000 sq. ft. of beachfront event space, Islander Resort is an iconic destination for weddings, corporate retreats, and special gatherings - with multiple venue offerings like the newly designed Islander House conference center, The Great Lawn, Coral Pointe, and Tideline Terrace.

Islander Resort also brings five unique dining experiences that celebrate the flavors of the Keys:

  • Drift on the Beach – A coastal-inspired restaurant serving fresh seafood, locally sourced grilled specialties, incredible smash burgers, and breakfast favorites with stunning ocean views.

  • Tides Beachside Bar – Casual and inviting, with Florida Mango Salsa, smoky Jerk Chicken Wings, and tropical cocktails.

  • Coccoloba – A vibrant oceanfront eatery offering fresh fish tacos, lobster rolls, and refreshing beverages.

  • Sandy’s – A thatched-roof beach bar with laid-back charm and signature island drinks.

  • Seaside Sports – Grab-and-go snacks and refreshments for adventurers on the move.

Honoring its surroundings, Islander Resort integrates eco-conscious initiatives that protect the delicate Florida Keys ecosystem. The resort features the largest solar installation in Islamorada, an on-site water reclamation system for irrigation, and energy-efficient upgrades.

Deeply rooted in the local community, the resort partners with Islamorada Beer Company, boo kee sue botanics, and local artist Jul Jankowski, whose work is showcased throughout the property. Guests can also contribute to reef conservation through Reef Relief activations, supporting efforts to preserve the region’s coral reefs.

To experience the new Islander Resort, visit www.islanderfloridakeys.com or call 305-664-2031
