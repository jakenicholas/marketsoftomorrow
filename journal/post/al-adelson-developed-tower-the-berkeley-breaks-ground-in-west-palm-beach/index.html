Al Adelson-developed tower, The Berkeley breaks ground in West Palm Beach

West Palm Beach’s quickly-growing skyline is set to welcome its newest luxury addition: The Berkeley Palm Beach , a 26-story waterfront...

By Jake Nicholas January 21, 2025

West Palm Beach’s quickly-growing skyline is set to welcome its newest luxury addition: The Berkeley Palm Beach, a 26-story waterfront condominium by Al Adelson and Morrison family’s Sympatico Real Estate, the visionary developer behind The Bristol. In December 2024, The Berkeley marked a major milestone with a groundbreaking ceremony at its 1.92-acre site at 621 Clearwater Park Rd, overlooking Clear Lake. The event also celebrated the opening of its sales gallery on the 9th floor of 500 South Australian Avenue, where private appointments are now available.

Nestled along the shores of Clear Lake, The Berkeley offers a unique combination of waterfront living and urban accessibility. Located just steps from the growing Cultural District, the development ensures convenient access to I-95, Palm Beach International Airport, Brightline and Tri-Rail trains, and vibrant downtown destinations like The Kravis Center, CityPlace, Clematis Street, and the waterfront. 

Designed by Miami-based Arquitectonica and built by John Moriarty & Associates, The Berkeley replaces a former office site with a bold vision of modern luxury. Every unit has a water view facing Clear Lake, with upper floors adding vistas of downtown West Palm Beach, the Intracoastal Waterway, and the Atlantic Ocean.

The Berkeley features 193 carefully crafted residences ranging from 1,700 to 5,000 square feet, with prices starting at $1.9 million. Floorplans include two- to five-bedroom layouts, each equipped with Snaidero-crafted Italian kitchens, Sub-Zero and Wolf appliances, and the largest private balconies in West Palm Beach, offering seamless indoor-outdoor living.

The Berkeley’s amenity spaces are thoughtfully designed to enhance the lifestyle of its residents. The seventh-floor deck includes a resort-style pool, spa, fitness center, and family-friendly features like teen and children’s play areas. For an elevated experience, the rooftop deck offers an adult-only pool, a private dining room, and a sophisticated club lounge.

Under the guidance of Al Adelson, The Berkeley continues a tradition of excellence in West Palm Beach real estate. With over 50 years of experience, Adelson is recognized for his transformative developments, including The Bristol, which set new standards for luxury in the city. His work at The Berkeley reflects a meticulous approach to quality and design.

Beyond real estate, Adelson is an active member of the West Palm Beach community, serving on the boards of the Kravis Center and Cleveland Clinic Florida, which is preparing to expand its presence downtown with both a new hospital and office.

As West Palm Beach continues to attract new development and residents, The Berkeley stands out as a symbol of the city’s evolution. Alongside other high-profile projects like South Flagler House and Alba Palm Beach, The Berkeley reinforces the area’s growing reputation as a destination for luxury living.

Set to be completed in 2027, The Berkeley Palm Beach is poised to become a defining presence in West Palm Beach’s rapidly changing skyline.
