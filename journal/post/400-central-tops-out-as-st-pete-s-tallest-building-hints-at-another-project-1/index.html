Inside look at the tallest residential building on Florida's Gulf Coast, The Residences at 400 Central

St. Petersburg’s skyline has transformed with the rise of The Residences at 400 Central, now standing as the tallest residential building...

By Jake Nicholas October 22, 2024

St. Petersburg’s skyline has transformed with the rise of The Residences at 400 Central, now standing as the tallest residential building on Florida’s Gulf Coast at 46 stories and 515 feet. This impressive structure has already made a mark on the city’s urban landscape, setting a new standard for high-end living downtown.

Renderings by: Red Apple Group

While construction continues toward an expected mid-2025 completion date, The Residences at 400 Central is quickly shaping up to be more than just a residential tower. The project, which offers 301 luxury condos, ground-floor retail, and premium office space, reflects a larger vision for the future of urban living in St. Pete. Its sleek, modern design comes courtesy of Arquitectonica, enhancing the city’s downtown with a bold new look.

Renderings by: Red Apple Group

John Catsimatidis' passion for St. Petersburg is brought to life in his bold vision for The Residences at 400 Central, a development that aims to honor the charm of the coastal city his family fell in love with decades ago. Rising 515 feet on Central Avenue, this live-work-play destination is poised to become the tallest building in Pinellas County. Designed by renowned architect Bernardo Fort-Brescia of Arquitectonica, the building reflects the unique landscape where land meets water. The team behind 400 Central sought to capture the essence of St. Pete's vibrant downtown and waterfront, creating a striking addition to the city’s skyline that will connect St. Petersburg to a global roster of cities known for Arquitectonica’s modernist designs.

Beyond its iconic architecture, 400 Central offers residents a blend of luxury, convenience, and entertainment through more than 35,000 square feet of curated interior and exterior amenities. Designed by Vincent Celano of Celano Design Studio, known for its collaborations with top hospitality brands, the spaces feature a warm, coastal-modern aesthetic. Amenities include a resort-style pool deck, private co-working spaces, a grand lobby, and a theater lounge. At the pinnacle of the tower, the Sky Lounge Observatory offers floor-to-ceiling glass windows and a glass elevator, providing breathtaking views from Tampa Bay to the Gulf of Mexico, delivering an unmatched living experience on Florida’s west coast.

Renderings by: Red Apple Group

What truly makes 400 Central stand out is the lifestyle it promises. Residents will enjoy 36,000 square feet of private indoor and outdoor amenities, creating a resort-like atmosphere right in the heart of the city. Highlights include:

  • Sky Lounge Observatory: A 46th-floor glass-enclosed space offering sweeping panoramic views of the city and beyond.

  • Resort-Style Pool Deck: A spacious 7th-floor deck with a heated pool, spa, bocce court, fire pits, and outdoor dining areas for relaxing or socializing.

  • State-of-the-Art Fitness Center: Designed to accommodate both indoor and outdoor workouts, this fitness space provides flexibility and top-tier equipment.

  • Entertainment and Social Areas: From a game room and theater to private co-working spaces and a boardroom, these spaces cater to a variety of lifestyles and needs.

Rendering by: Red Apple Group

In addition to the luxurious amenities, the building will house 60,000 square feet of retail and 45,000 square feet of Class A office space, making it a dynamic mixed-use development that will cater to both residents and the wider community.

As anticipation builds, buyers are expected to begin closing on their condos by summer 2025. For those looking to be part of this game-changing development, the opportunity to secure a spot in St. Pete’s tallest residential building is drawing near.
