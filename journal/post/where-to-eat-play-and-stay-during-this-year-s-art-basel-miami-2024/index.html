Miami

Where to eat, play, and stay during this year's Miami Art Week and Art Basel Miami 2024

As Miami Art Week (Sunday, Dec 1, 2024 – Sunday, Dec 8, 2024) approaches, the city transforms into a vibrant hub of culture, art, and...

By Jake Nicholas December 2, 2024

As Miami Art Week (Sunday, Dec 1, 2024 – Sunday, Dec 8, 2024) approaches, the city transforms into a vibrant hub of culture, art, and entertainment with visitors from all around the world. Whether you're attending Art Basel or exploring the city's many art-driven events, there's no shortage of exceptional places to eat, play, and stay.

Estiatorio Milos

Estiatorio Milos, founded in 1979 by Greek restaurateur Costas Spiliadis, is a family-owned, globally celebrated Greek restaurant empire. Deeply rooted in Greek culture and traditions, Milos specializes in authentic cuisine crafted from impeccably sourced ingredients, including fresh catches from local fishermen and produce from Mediterranean and local markets. Known for its seasonal specialties, Milos proudly celebrates stone crab season, showcasing this delicacy alongside its renowned seafood offerings. With locations in Montreal, New York, Miami, London, Athens, Dubai, Los Cabos, Singapore, Palm Beach, and Toronto, Milos is set to expand to West Palm Beach in coming months, with Los Angeles in the coming year.

Lafayette

Lafayette, a creation by Mr. Hospitality Miami, seamlessly merges the classic allure of an American steakhouse with the refined elegance of a London speakeasy. Led by Michelin-starred Executive Chef Kylian Goussot, the menu highlights dry-aged USDA prime cuts, fresh seafood, and bold, ingredient-driven flavors. Complementing its culinary mastery, Lafayette boasts an impressive selection of prestigious wines and luxury spirits, promising an elevated dining experience during Miami’s most vibrant week.

Art of the Party

Ocean Drive’s annual Art of the Party returns on Thursday, December 5, from 8 PM to 11 PM, at the iconic Ocean & La Côte Lawn at Fontainebleau Miami Beach (4441 Collins Ave). This highly anticipated Miami Art Week event celebrates the release of the magazine’s December Art Issue, featuring cover star Chance The Rapper. The night kicks off with a high-energy DJ set by DJ Crespo, followed by an unforgettable performance by Chance The Rapper. Italian DJ Gianluca Vacchi will close the evening with his signature blend of house, techno, and tropical beats, making this a must-attend celebration.

E11EVEN Miami

E11EVEN Miami, the iconic ultraclub known for its celebrity allure, unmatched theatrics, luxury, and top-tier entertainment, is elevating Miami Art Week with The Art of Nightlife and its inaugural Golf Classic. From Wednesday, December 4, to Sunday, December 8, guests can expect unforgettable performances from headliners like Steve Aoki and 50 Cent, along with surprise acts.

On Thursday, December 5, the club debuts the E11EVEN Golf Classic at Normandy Shores Golf Club. Sponsored by Happy Dad, Maestro Dobel Tequila, and E11EVEN Vodka, the event features a day of golf, premium drinks, exclusive art activations, and a charitable Longest Drive Contest. Tickets and table reservations are available at 11miami.com.

Michelangelo Exhibition VIP Vernissage

On Monday, December 2, from 7:00 PM to 9:30 PM, the Sagamore Hotel South Beach will host the exclusive Michelangelo Exhibition VIP Vernissage, presented by BitBasel, MOA Miami, and Treasure Investment Corp. The Eternal Creations will unveil seven pure silver casts of Michelangelo Buonarroti’s most iconic works, each authenticated through their direct connection to the original molds preserved by Florence’s historic Fonderia Artistica Ferdinando Marinelli.

Guests will enjoy wine, curated culinary offerings, and live performances, with artist Yvette Lopez serving as Mistress of Ceremony. These one-of-a-kind masterpieces, crafted in limited editions of silver, gold, and platinum, debut during Miami Art Week, celebrating a legacy safeguarded since the Vatican’s treasures were entrusted to the foundry by Pope Pius XI before World War II.

ZZ's Club ZZ's Club, located in the Miami Design District, is a two-story Japanese restaurant and private membership club. The first floor boasts two dining rooms, a Japanese sports bar, a marble sushi counter serving omakase with seafood sourced from Tokyo, Wagyu, and Kobe beef, along with a lush outdoor courtyard. Upstairs, members can enjoy exclusive features such as a cocktail bar, an outdoor cigar lounge, and a backgammon terrace.

Mansion Yacht House

Mansion Yacht House (MYH), Miami’s newest ultra-exclusive floating private club, is redefining luxury with a curated membership experience for visionaries, entrepreneurs, and artists. Unveiling during Miami Art Week, the 9,000-square-foot masterpiece offers an inspiring blend of elegance, innovation, and connection across three stunning decks. Highlights include the tranquil Main Deck Living Room, the vibrant Art Room fostering creativity, and the SOBO Upper Deck for sun-soaked relaxation and scenic views. Docked at 4525 Collins Avenue near the Eden Rock Hotel, MYH promises a “home away from home on the water,” setting a new standard in Miami’s luxury scene.

Sweet Liberty Drinks & Supply Co.

Renowned for its award-winning mixology and celebrated for its inventive cocktails, this iconic bar has become a favorite among locals and visitors alike. With its electric atmosphere, creative libations, and welcoming hospitality, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking to immerse themselves in a vibrant, cocktail-fueled nightlife experience that leaves a lasting impression.

Faena Hotel Miami Beach

During Miami Art Week, the Faena District stands as a dynamic epicenter, attracting global visitors to Miami Beach’s thriving arts, culture, design, and collaborative scene. At the heart of it all is Faena Hotel Miami Beach, the first luxury resort to earn the prestigious Forbes five-star rating in its inaugural year. With iconic features like Damien Hirst’s Golden Mammoth by the pool, a stunning red-and-gold lobby, and opulent interiors, the hotel offers a truly unique and unforgettable experience.

The Setai Miami Beach

For Art Basel Miami 2024, The Setai stands as a beacon of global hospitality excellence, seamlessly blending Asian warmth with unmatched personalized service, refined dining, and luxurious comfort. Designed by Jean-Michel Gathy and Jaya Ibrahim, this iconic property is the only Miami Beach hotel honored with both the AAA Five Diamond designation and a Forbes Five-Star rating, alongside two Michelin Keys. Enhancing its South Asian allure, the hotel recently introduced Japón, crafted by Saladino Design Studios and chefs Vijayudu Veena and Iván Monzón, joining its celebrated dining venues, Jaya and Ocean Grill, to deliver an elevated culinary experience during Art Week.
