A $45.5M penthouse in downtown Tampa just hit the market

By Jake Nicholas June 3, 2026

The premier penthouse in downtown Tampa just hit the market. Pendry Residences Tampa has officially released Penthouse South, a $45.5 million, two-story home perched on the 38th floor of the under-construction Pendry Hotel & Residences Tampa tower along the Hillsborough River — one of only two penthouse residences in the entire building.

The numbers tell the story. Penthouse South spans more than 10,000 square feet of interior space — 10,072 square feet, to be exact — with six bedrooms, seven full bathrooms, and two partial baths, plus over 1,800 square feet of outdoor space on the main level and another 2,592 square feet of rooftop terrace. The residence sits atop one of the tallest buildings in downtown Tampa, on the tower's southern side, with primarily western exposure plus south and southwest water views oriented for sunsets over Tampa Bay. Completion for both the hotel and the residences is targeted for 2027.

The tower itself is developed by Two Roads Development with architecture by Miami-based Arquitectonica and interiors by Toronto-based Studio Munge. The 38-story building will hold 200 private residences across floors 15 to 38, sitting above a 220-key Pendry Tampa hotel on the lower floors. Penthouse South receives stepped-up finishes that distinguish it from the rest of the building — custom millwork, curated wall coverings, luminous marble, white oak, and the sculptural forms that have become a signature of acclaimed designer Alessandro Munge.

The experience begins at a private elevator that opens directly into an entry foyer, leading to a living room appointed with a grand piano and a bar, framed by 10-foot ceilings and floor-to-ceiling windows. The kitchen anchors the home as both a functional and social centerpiece — a marble island, custom cabinetry, and a full suite of Wolf and Sub-Zero appliances — connecting seamlessly to the adjoining family room.

The home is built for a fully programmed lifestyle. Plans include a private theater with a snack station, a dedicated home office, a fitness room, and a spa. The primary suite occupies its own private wing with a separate terrace, dual walk-in closets, and an en suite featuring a soaking tub and walk-in shower. A grand staircase rises through the two-story residence to the rooftop.

The rooftop terrace is the headline. The 2,592-square-foot deck delivers a fully equipped outdoor living environment high above the city — a private pool, a hot tub, an outdoor bar, a summer kitchen, a dining area, a tea garden, a full bathroom, and an outdoor shower, all framed by panoramic skyline and bay views.

Beyond the residence, ownership at Pendry Residences Tampa unlocks a fully serviced lifestyle. Residents have a private lobby and valet drop-off, dedicated elevators, an owners' lounge, residential staff, and their own rooftop residential pool — plus full access to the Pendry Tampa hotel's amenities, including a 20,000-square-foot Spa Pendry, more than 10,000 square feet of conference and ballroom space, a fitness center, a children's club, and a series of restaurants, cafés, and shops along the Riverwalk. Property management and hospitality service are handled by Montage Hotels & Resorts.

The location anchors the entire pitch. The Pendry tower rises at the point where the Hillsborough River meets Tampa Bay, directly on the Tampa Riverwalk — a continuously expanding waterfront promenade that has become downtown Tampa's defining public amenity. With construction climbing toward the 15th floor and both the hotel and residences set for completion in 2027, Penthouse South enters the market as one of the most substantial condominium offerings in Tampa Bay — and a clear marker of how far the city's luxury ceiling has risen.
