The Luxury Collection debuts first resort on Rhodes

The Luxury Collection has made its Rhodes debut with the opening of Amoh, a Luxury Collection Resort, Rhodes, introducing Marriott International’s luxury brand to one of Greece’s most historic island destinations.

By Kait Nicholas May 6, 2026

The Luxury Collection has made its Rhodes debut with the opening of Amoh, a Luxury Collection Resort, Rhodes, introducing Marriott International’s luxury brand to one of Greece’s most historic island destinations.

Set on a secluded peninsula at Kavos Beach in Pefki, just outside the historic town of Lindos, the resort overlooks the Mediterranean and a historic limestone quarry, placing it within one of the island’s more geologically and historically layered coastal settings.

The resort includes 197 rooms and suites, spanning sea-view accommodations and private pool categories. The Rhoda Presidential Suite offers a separate living area, an expansive terrace, and direct access to a private pool.

Interiors were led by London-based Studio Lost, drawing from Greece’s stonework traditions and the movement of the surrounding sea. A restrained palette of mineral tones and natural materials carries throughout, with sculptural stone elements and a curated art program referencing the nearby quarry and local craftsmanship.

Dining is organized across eight restaurants and bars. Lithos serves as the main restaurant with a European menu shaped by Mediterranean flavors, while Cava di Pietra focuses on Italian cuisine in an open-air setting. Glaze centers on premium cuts, and Kymata presents a contemporary take on Greek seafood. Additional venues include the AMOH Lounge, Isle Pool Bar, AMOHNITE Beach Bar, and Oniro Patisserie, which highlights traditional Greek pastries and ice creams.

Culinary programming continues beyond the restaurants with a daily olive grove tasting featuring regional varieties, alongside a weekly sunset aperitivo with small bites and drinks served around a firepit overlooking the coastline.

Two sandy beaches, Kavos and Kymata, are accessible by foot from the resort, connecting guests directly to the shoreline.

Wellness is centered around Elispa, where treatments incorporate olive oil, citrus extracts, mineral salts, and local botanicals. The spa includes six treatment rooms, a steam bath, sauna, and a dedicated hair and nail salon. Fitness facilities include a roughly 2,400-square-foot Technogym center, a Reformer Pilates studio with virtual training, tennis courts, and both indoor and outdoor pools.

The opening adds Rhodes to The Luxury Collection’s portfolio in Greece, which also includes properties in Athens, Santorini, Mykonos, Crete, and Paros.
