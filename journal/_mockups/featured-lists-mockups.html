Mockup B

Tabbed switcher

One section, two modes. Gold pill toggle at the top of the head column lets users flip between editorial picks and city markets. Saves vertical real estate, keeps the same component, and gives both treatments equal billing. Tradeoff: a tab the user has to discover — if "By Market" isn't selected, those landing pages get zero pixels.

Mockup C

Two stacked rows under one header

Single section header ("Featured"), two horizontal scrollers stacked vertically with subtle dividers — Netflix-style. Editorial picks on top in the larger 300×380 cards, markets below in tighter 200-wide tiles. Both visible at once, each with its own scroll behavior. Tradeoff: takes more vertical room than tabbed (B), but communicates "two parallel ideas" without making users pick.

Mockup D

Magazine — Editorial hero left, Markets rail right

Print-style 2-column layout. One big editorial hero on the left (rotates through Featured Lists), compact city rail on the right showing 4-5 markets with thumbnails + counts. Feels intentional and curated — both items have equal real estate but different roles. Tradeoff: fewer items visible at once than the carousel layouts; better for "front door" emphasis than "scroll-through discovery."
